# Enterprise Website Design & Business Guidelines

## Objective

Build a world-class B2B website that combines **Products + Services** and creates the perception of an established, trusted, enterprise technology company.

The website must communicate credibility, expertise, execution capability, and measurable business outcomes—not just attractive visuals.

---

## 1. Business Positioning

The website must clearly answer:

- Who are we?
- What business problems do we solve?
- Why should clients choose us?
- Why now?
- Why are we better than competitors?
- What measurable outcomes do we deliver?

Focus on business value rather than technology features.

---

## 2. Trust Principles

Every page should increase trust.

Include:

- Customer logos
- Case studies
- Client testimonials
- Success metrics
- Certifications
- Technology partners
- Awards
- Security practices
- Compliance
- Team expertise
- Public GitHub/Open Source contributions
- Industry recognition

Avoid generic marketing claims. Every claim should be supported with evidence.

---

## 3. Products + Services

Clearly separate **Products** and **Services**.

**Products**

- SaaS
- Platforms
- AI Tools
- Accelerators
- APIs

**Services**

- AI Consulting
- Product Engineering
- Platform Engineering
- Cloud
- DevOps
- Security
- Digital Transformation

Explain how products accelerate services.

---

## 4. Enterprise Messaging

Never sell technology. Sell outcomes.

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---------|------|
| "We build websites." | "We reduce engineering costs by automating digital operations." |

---

## 5. Customer Journey

Guide visitors through:

```
Problem → Business Impact → Solution → Proof → Process → Pricing/Engagement → Contact
```

Every page should have a clear CTA.

---

## 6. Social Proof

Show (real numbers only):

- Projects delivered
- Years of experience
- Countries served
- Industries served
- Technologies supported
- Customer satisfaction
- Retention rate
- Response time
- Deployment success rate

---

## 7. Case Studies

Every case study should include:

- Client challenge
- Solution
- Technology
- Timeline
- Results
- Business impact
- Metrics
- Client testimonial

---

## 8. Portfolio

Show:

- Screenshots
- Architecture
- Technology stack
- Before vs After
- Performance improvements
- ROI

Not just images.

---

## 9. Technical Credibility

Demonstrate expertise through:

- Architecture diagrams
- Engineering blogs
- Open source projects
- API documentation
- SDKs
- Developer resources
- Benchmarks
- Security documentation

---

## 10. Product Pages

Each product should include:

- Overview
- Features
- Benefits
- Screenshots
- Integrations
- Pricing
- FAQ
- Documentation
- Demo
- API Reference
- Security
- Roadmap

---

## 11. Service Pages

Each service should explain:

- Business problem
- Process
- Deliverables
- Technologies
- Timeline
- Pricing model
- Case studies
- Expected outcomes

---

## 12. Design Principles

Use:

- Large whitespace
- Strong typography
- Professional illustrations
- High-quality animations (respect `prefers-reduced-motion`)
- Consistent spacing (design tokens)
- Minimal color palette
- Premium interactions

Avoid visual clutter.

---

## 13. User Experience

Navigation should require minimal effort.

Support:

- Global Search (future)
- Breadcrumbs
- Sticky Navigation
- Fast Loading
- Mobile First
- Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA)
- Keyboard Navigation

---

## 14. Marketing

Support:

- SEO
- Landing Pages
- Blogs
- Resources
- Webinars
- Whitepapers
- News
- Newsletter
- Lead Magnets
- Email Capture

---

## 15. SEO

Every page must include:

- Optimized Title
- Meta Description
- Canonical URL (matches deploy environment)
- OpenGraph
- Twitter Card
- Structured Data (JSON-LD)
- Internal Links
- Schema.org types appropriate to page

See `.ai/seo/checklist.md`.

---

## 16. Conversion Optimization

CTAs to use:

- Book Discovery Call
- Schedule Demo
- Download Brochure
- Contact Sales
- Start Free Trial
- View Case Studies

---

## 17. Performance

Target:

- Lighthouse ≥ 95
- Core Web Vitals Pass
- Mobile Optimized
- Image Optimization
- Lazy Loading
- CDN
- Static Generation (`output: 'export'`)

---

## 18. Accessibility

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA:

- Keyboard Navigation
- Screen Readers
- Color Contrast
- Focus States
- Semantic HTML

---

## 19. Security & Compliance

Highlight only what is true:

- Secure Development Lifecycle
- Data Privacy
- GDPR
- SOC 2 (if applicable)
- ISO 27001 (if applicable)
- Vulnerability Management
- Security Testing

---

## 20. Content Guidelines

Write for executives. Focus on:

- Business outcomes
- ROI
- Cost savings
- Time savings
- Risk reduction
- Scalability
- Reliability

Avoid excessive technical jargon on marketing pages.

---

## 21. Enterprise Feel

Reinforce:

- Stability
- Innovation
- Scale
- Reliability
- Global capability
- Engineering excellence
- Long-term partnership

---

## 22. AI Content Rules

Never generate fake:

- Customers
- Reviews
- Testimonials
- Certifications
- Awards
- Revenue
- Team members
- Project counts
- Partnerships

Use placeholders until real information is available.

See `.ai/content/ai-content-rules.md`.
